Schrödinger's Equation

Schrödinger’s Equation helps us understand how electrons move inside atoms. It is mainly used for atoms with only one electron, like hydrogen. The equation helps us figure out the different energy levels the electron can have. Instead of thinking of the electron as a tiny particle, Schrödinger thought of electrons as a wave. The wave shows where the electron is most likely to be in the atom, but it doesn’t show an exact spot. This idea works well for simple atoms, but it's harder for bigger atoms because they have more electrons and complicated interactions.

Year: 1926

Created by: Erwin Schrödinger, an Austrian physicist, born in Austria.
