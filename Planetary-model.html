Planetary Model

The Planetary Model says that electrons move around the center of an atom at the nucleus, like planets orbiting the Sun. These paths are called orbits. Electrons stay in one of the orbits, and their energy stays the same while they are there. Electrons can absorb or release energy as light when they move to different orbits.

Year: 1911

Created by: Ernest Rutherford, born in New Zealand.
