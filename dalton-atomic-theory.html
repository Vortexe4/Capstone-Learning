Dalton's Atomic Theory

1. All matter is composed of atoms, which cannot be split or destroyed.
2. Atoms of the same element are identical in terms of size, mass, and properties, while atoms of different elements are distinct.
3. Atoms from different elements combine in fixed, simple whole-number ratios to form compounds.
4. Atoms remain unchanged when a compound breaks down; they are not created or destroyed.
5. During chemical reactions, atoms are rearranged, combined, or separated.

Year: 1803

Created by: John Dalton (1766-1844), born in Cumberland, England.
